Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture used in modern computers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches peak emissions and begins major investments in renewable energy and chemical abatement.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of the RISE strategy focused on responsible, inclusive, and sustainable innovation.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, dramatically lowering carbon emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together leaders to collaborate on sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
